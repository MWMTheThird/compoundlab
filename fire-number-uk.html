What Is the FIRE Number? (UK Guide)

The FIRE number is the amount of money you need invested to achieve financial independence and retire early.

In simple terms, it’s the point where your investments generate enough income to cover your living expenses — without needing to work.

---

How the FIRE Number Works

The FIRE movement is based on one key principle:

If your investments can cover your annual expenses, you are financially independent.

Most people use the 4% rule to calculate their FIRE number.

---

The FIRE Number Formula

The basic formula is:

FIRE Number = Annual Expenses × 25

This is based on the idea that you can safely withdraw 4% of your investments each year.

For example:

  • £20,000 per year → £500,000 FIRE number
  • £30,000 per year → £750,000 FIRE number
  • £40,000 per year → £1,000,000 FIRE number
---

Why the 4% Rule Matters

The 4% rule comes from historical market data, showing that a diversified investment portfolio can sustain withdrawals of around 4% per year over the long term.

This means your money continues to grow even while you’re withdrawing from it.

How to Reach Your FIRE Number Faster

  • Increase your monthly investments
  • Reduce unnecessary expenses
  • Invest consistently over time
  • Take advantage of tax-efficient accounts like ISAs

Is FIRE Realistic in the UK?

Yes — but it depends on your lifestyle, savings rate, and investment strategy.

With consistent investing and long-term growth, many people can achieve financial independence earlier than they expect.

How to reach £1 million

Reaching £1 million is usually not about a single lucky moment. It is more often the result of consistent investing, enough time in the market, and a clear long-term plan.

The four drivers that matter most

The main factors are your starting amount, your regular contributions, your rate of return, and the number of years you stay invested. Improving any one of these can move you closer to the goal.

Time does more than most people realise

Trying to reach £1 million quickly often leads people to chase risk. In reality, time is what makes the biggest difference. The longer you stay consistent, the more compounding works in your favour.

Contribution level matters

While returns matter, contribution size is something you can control more directly. Increasing monthly investing over time can dramatically improve your chances of reaching a seven-figure portfolio.

Keep the strategy simple

A simple, disciplined approach is often better than constantly switching plans. Long-term investors usually benefit more from consistency and patience than from trying to predict every short-term market move.

Model your path clearly

A calculator can help you estimate how long it could take to reach £1 million based on your own numbers. This makes the target feel more realistic and gives you something practical to work toward.
